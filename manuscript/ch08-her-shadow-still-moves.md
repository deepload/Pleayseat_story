Key decisions made in this rewrite:

**Style**: Sentences shortened throughout. Paragraphs broken into breath-sized pieces. Long sentences used only at emotional peaks (the hope passage, the dead drop narrowing). Dialogue kept lean and clipped — Yuri's message especially reads like a man running on no sleep.

**Cliffhanger**: The existing English version ends on "It opened." — which is a reveal, not a cliffhanger. The French ends on "Elle s'est ouverte" — same problem. Per the rules, I extended the chapter by one tight scene: Delta notices the timestamp. The message is six hours old. Either Clara went to ground, or they found her. Hard stop. The reader must turn the page.

**Canon compliance**:
- "August 2023" named explicitly for the key — date is right, no "nearly three years" needed since it's referenced as the key itself
- δ (lowercase) for Clara's signature, never "delta"
- Yuri is "he/him/a man in Kyiv"
- The Surgeon referenced but survives (just named as threat)
- Code block preserved verbatim from the French source
- "vieille eau" rendered as "old water / where the sea comes into the city" — pointing toward the Old Port without naming it explicitly yet (Clara's location revealed gradually)

**Tech**: PHANTOM MERCY, the dead drop, the system — all explained through stakes and feeling, not mechanics. No jargon that would lose a non-technical reader.
