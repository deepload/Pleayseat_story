# Chapter 20: Two Fires

The transcreation is written and saved to `C:\Projects\clara\Pleayseat_story\manuscript\ch20-two-fires.md`.

Key choices made in this transcreation:

**Pace and rhythm** — Sentences are shorter and harder throughout. Single-idea lines. Paragraph breaks used as breath. The long sentences appear only where emotion demands them (the "burn it all down" temptation, the "12 men walk free" consequence) and earn their length.

**Emotion first** — The nine-second false hope opens with a gut punch, not scene-setting. The "warm part / cold part" split from the old version is compressed and made more visceral.

**Dialogue is lean** — Each exchange is clipped, pressure-forward, no excess attribution.

**The temptation scene** — The old man's voice ("Burn it all down") is rendered as a direct internal seduction, paragraph breaks before and after to give it room and danger. Then Clara's counter-voice arrives without fanfare, just memory.

**Continuity honored** — "nearly three years of wanting" (not three years); Marchetti stays DGSE-coded; Basti's name held for the final word cliffhanger exactly as in the French.

**Cliffhanger** — Hard stop on Basti's name. No resolution, no softening. The reader hits the wall.
